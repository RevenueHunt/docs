# A Quiz Playbook for Fashion & Apparel Brands

Style is personal and fit is the number-one reason fashion gets returned. A quiz captures a shopper's taste, size, and the occasion they're shopping for, then recommends pieces that actually suit them, instead of leaving them to scroll a grid and guess.

This playbook is built from **101 real fashion and apparel quizzes** running on RevenueHunt. Fashion has a distinctive profile: the **highest average order value of any vertical**, paired with the **lowest first-session conversion**, which changes what the quiz is really for.

## Read the conversion number in context

Fashion's median conversion is **4%**, lower than beauty or supplements, and a quarter of fashion quizzes show **0%** attributed first-session conversion. That isn't the quiz failing. Apparel is a considered, compare-and-come-back purchase: shoppers browse, leave, think, and buy later, often on a different device. Two things follow:

1. **The lead matters as much as the instant sale.** Capture the email and the style profile so you can bring them back. A "0% same-session" quiz that captured 200 segmented leads is doing its job.
2. **Each conversion is worth more.** At a **$101 median order** (rising to **$314** for the top 10%), fashion has the highest AOV of any vertical, so a lower conversion rate still produces strong revenue per completion when the order lands.

## The benchmark build

The top fashion quizzes by revenue share this shape:

!!! success "What the top fashion quizzes have in common"
    - **7 questions**, around **3.8 answer choices** each
    - A **complete-look recommendation** (the piece plus what pairs with it): **75%** recommend a set
    - **Branching logic** in most, **62%** of top performers, the highest of any vertical
    - Email leaning **none or optional**, with the lead captured where it fits the brand
    - Often a broad results set: the median is 4 products, but top quizzes route a deep catalog with branching first

Branching is the fashion signature. Departments, body types, and occasions diverge so sharply that a single linear path can't serve everyone, so the best fashion quizzes split shoppers early, then recommend within the right lane.

!!! tip "Where to push"

## The questions that matter

Top fashion quizzes ask about taste, fit, and occasion, each tied to a job (the [data-worth-collecting](/customer-success/what-data-to-collect/) rule):

- **Style preference / "what vibe are you after?"** The aesthetic they gravitate to. The backbone of the recommendation and your segments.
- **Fit and size**, including the measurements that matter for your category. This is what reduces returns.
- **"What does a typical day look like for you?"** Occasion and use case (everyday, work, event) changes what you put in front of them.
- **Colours and materials** they love or won't wear, so you never recommend a dealbreaker.
- **Budget**, to keep recommendations in range.

A **"select all that apply"** multi-select is common for style and occasion, where shoppers rarely fit one box.

## Recommend complete looks, not single items

**75% of fashion quizzes recommend a set.** A single garment is a small order; a complete look is a basket:

- Use slots to recommend a **full outfit or capsule** (top, bottom, layer, accessory), each matched to the shopper. See the [bundles, kits & routines](/customer-success/recommend-bundles-kits/) playbook and [Set up recommendations](/how-to-guides/set-up-recommendations/).
- For broad catalogs, **[route with a funnel quiz](/how-to-guides/set-up-funnel-quiz/) first** (by department, say), then recommend within it, this is how the top fashion quizzes use their heavy branching.

## Cut returns with fit questions

Fit is the costliest problem in apparel, and the quiz is the cheapest place to fix it. Capturing size, shape, and fit preference up front means you recommend pieces that actually fit, so fewer shoppers buy the wrong size and send it back. Every avoided return is margin you keep, and it makes the high $101 AOV stick instead of bouncing.

---

## What the top and bottom quizzes look like

!!! success "📈 Best in class"
    A fashion store (~402 responses/180d) runs a **6-question** quiz with branching and **no email gate**, routing a 32-product catalog into a personalized profile that finishes at **100% completion**. It keeps the path short while covering a deep range, the fashion pattern of "branch wide, ask little."

!!! danger "📉 The fashion failure mode"
    A fashion store (~465 responses/180d) runs an 8-question quiz that finishes well but converts at **0%** in-session, with no email captured. The miss isn't the quiz length, it's walking away from the lead. In a category where shoppers buy later, an uncaptured email is the lost sale.

## Do / Don't

- **Do** capture the email and style profile. Fashion's low first-session conversion means the follow-up, not the session, is where many sales close.
- **Do** use fit and size questions to cut returns. Fewer returns is profit you keep on the highest AOV of any vertical.
- **Do** use branching to route a deep catalog, 62% of top fashion quizzes do, more than any other vertical.
- **Don't** judge the quiz on same-session conversion alone. Lead capture and return reduction are half its value here.
- **Don't** show a wall of options. A curated, on-style set converts better than endless choice.

## Frequently asked questions

### What questions does a fashion quiz need?

Style preference, fit and size (the measurements that matter for your category), occasion, colour and material preferences, and budget. Style and occasion often work as a "select all that apply".

### Why is my fashion quiz converting lower than beauty quizzes?

Because apparel is a considered, come-back-later purchase, fashion's median is 4% and a quarter show 0% same-session. That's expected. Capture the email and style profile so you convert the sale when it lands, and note fashion's AOV ($101 median) is the highest of any vertical.

### How does a quiz reduce returns?

Capturing fit and size up front means you recommend pieces that actually fit, so fewer shoppers buy the wrong size. Fewer returns is profit you keep.

### Should it recommend single items or full looks?

Full looks, 75% of quizzes do. Use slots to recommend a complete outfit or capsule, which turns a one-item visit into a basket.
